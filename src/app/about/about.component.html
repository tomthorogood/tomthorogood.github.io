About Me

Hello! I am Tom Thorogood. I am a fiction writer and software engineer in Seattle, WA.

My short fiction has appeared in Let the Weirdness In: A tribute to Kate Bush, Robbed of Sleep, Vol. 4 and The Corner Club Press, with more on the way!

I keep a copy of the Chicago Manual of Style on my desk, and I think indexing is a really fun exercise. I'm a paid subscriber to a dictionary website, which is one of my most-used sites. In other words: I love language, style, etymology, and lexicology.

By day, I am a software engineer for the University of Washington. Mostly I write python. You can find many of my projects on Github, including this site.

Some of my favorite software projects include an application-layer load balancer for netflow traffic, a live-updating workflow messenger for Slack, an Alexa app to tell me what fish I can catch in Animal Crossing at any given time, and a Zork-style adventure game as a commission for a friend's birthday.

I like to play games, and think a lot about game design. I love all all sorts of games, especially if they are non- (or only comically) violent.

I live in Seattle with my husband, Joel, and our sheltie, Rupert Giles.
